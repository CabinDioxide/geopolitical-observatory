核心论点

法国是"有选择的上游控制者"——在两个专业领域（核技术和国防/航空航天）拥有真正的上游主权，同时在广泛的消费和工业品上接受下游依赖。这是戴高乐式战略文化的有意选择——历史上优先核能+国防主权胜过消费品自给。核电（70%+电力来自国内反应堆）提供了2026年霍尔木兹危机中德国和日本都缺乏的结构性能源保险。但法国"欧洲战略自主"话语与现实之间的差距：所有阵风战机、梭鱼潜艇、阿斯特导弹的稀土磁铁依赖中国——这是戴高乐主义无法解决的结构空洞。尼日尔政变（2023）叠加萨赫勒撤军（2024）削弱了法国对西非关键矿产供应链的影响力，加速从非洲铀转向哈萨克铀+加拿大铀的过渡。这一转型速度将决定法国核能模式能否在2030年代维持其战略优势。

七、结构性评估

核能——50年产业政策的复利回报

法国在1970年代石油危机后坚持核能战略（Messmer计划，1974年），过去50年持续投资。2026年这一决策的结构性回报极为明显：法国成为欧洲能源危机的相对赢家。德国在2011年决定逐步退出核电，2022年后必须以高成本重建能源架构。法国展示了产缘政治的一个关键原则：战略性产业政策的价值在长期才能显现——决策者需要50年视野而非5年选举周期。

戴高乐主义的极限——为什么"欧洲战略自主"话语空洞

戴高乐主义的核心是"在关键战略领域独立于美国和苏联/俄罗斯"。核能 + 核武器 + 国防制造体现了这一原则。但在产缘政治时代，戴高乐主义的范围需要扩展——稀土、半导体、电池、AI芯片都成为"战略"领域。法国在这些新战略领域不是独立的，而是深度依赖中国或美国。话语与行动的差距是法国（以及欧盟整体）的信誉赤字。关闭这一差距需要法国（及欧盟）做出产业政策投入规模数量级的增加——目前未见。

France Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

法国供应链脆弱性评估

Risk Score: 42/100 — BALANCED

Core Thesis

France is "the selective upstream controller" — possessing genuine upstream sovereignty in two specialized domains (nuclear technology and defense/aerospace) while accepting downstream dependency across broad consumer and industrial goods. This is a Gaullist strategic culture's deliberate choice — historically prioritizing nuclear + defense sovereignty over consumer goods self-sufficiency. Nuclear (70%+ of electricity from domestic reactors) provides a structural energy insurance that Germany and Japan lacked in the 2026 Hormuz crisis. But the gap between France's "European strategic autonomy" rhetoric and reality: all Rafale fighters, Barracuda submarines, Aster missiles depend on Chinese rare earth magnets — a structural hollow Gaullism cannot resolve. Niger coup (2023) compounded with Sahel withdrawal (2024) weakened French influence over West African critical mineral chains, accelerating transition from African uranium to Kazakh + Canadian uranium. The speed of this transition will determine whether France's nuclear model can maintain its strategic advantage through the 2030s.

IV. Structural Transmission Chains

Chain 1: Nuclear's Energy Insulation Effect

France's 70%+ electricity from 56 nuclear reactors ↓ 2022 Russia-Ukraine war → European energy crisis ↓ German industrial electricity 2-3x surge, France's rising but far below neighbors ↓ France becomes net power exporter in European energy crisis (to Germany etc.) ↓ 2026 Hormuz blockade → gas prices surge across Europe ↓ France again relatively less damaged (nuclear dominant in electricity) ↓ This structural advantage is the 50-year compounding return of Gaullist-era energy policy

VII. Structural Assessment

Nuclear — The Compounding Return of 50-Year Industrial Policy

France stuck with nuclear strategy after the 1970s oil crisis (Messmer Plan, 1974), consistently investing for 50 years. In 2026 the structural return is extremely clear: France becomes the relative winner of the European energy crisis. Germany decided to phase out nuclear in 2011; post-2022 must rebuild energy architecture at high cost. France demonstrates a key principle of supply chain geopolitics: the value of strategic industrial policy only appears in the long term — policymakers need 50-year horizons rather than 5-year electoral cycles.

The Limit of Gaullism — Why "European Strategic Autonomy" Rhetoric Is Hollow

The core of Gaullism is "independence from the US and Soviet Union/Russia in key strategic domains." Nuclear + nuclear weapons + defense manufacturing embody this principle. But in the supply chain geopolitics era, Gaullism's scope needs to expand — rare earths, semiconductors, batteries, AI chips all become "strategic" domains. France is not independent in these new strategic domains but deeply dependent on China or US. The rhetoric-action gap is France's (and EU's overall) credibility deficit. Closing this gap requires France (and EU) to make order-of-magnitude increases in industrial policy investment — not yet visible.
