美国供应链脆弱性评估（2026）

United States Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

风险评分：68/100 — 净脆弱 / NET VULNERABLE

核心论点

美国是一个"上端帝国+中端空洞"——在设计（EDA、芯片架构、AI模型）、国防系统、能源商品、农产品上占据全球顶峰，但在把这些设计变成物理产品所需的中端制造环节结构性空洞。具体而言：华盛顿规定全球技术管制的规则，同时无法制造自己AI产业需要的先进芯片；它拥有全球最强军事投射能力，而F-35依赖中国稀土磁铁。能源独立已基本实现，但矿物加工和芯片制造的空洞期（2025-2030）与供应链武器化的高峰期（2027-2032）完全重合。CHIPS法案520亿美元和IRA 3690亿美元的产业政策是迟到的承认，但不足以在该空洞期被利用之前关闭空洞。这种"规则制定者同时是其规则无法解决问题的受害者"的结构是美国战略的核心悖论。

一、结构性画像：上端帝国与中端空洞

美国的产业地位呈现一个独特的双峰分布：

  • 顶峰：设计与最终整合——EDA（Synopsys+Cadence合计~60%市场）、芯片设计（Nvidia、AMD、Apple、Broadcom、Qualcomm）、AI模型（OpenAI、Anthropic、Google、Meta）、国防系统（RTX、洛克希德、诺斯罗普）、AI软件栈
  • 谷底：中游制造——先进芯片代工（TSMC Arizona产能不足本土需求的20%）、关键矿产精炼（90%依赖进口加工）、电池、太阳能、大宗工业化学品、精密机械零部件
  • 基础：上游资源——能源基本自给（页岩革命）、粮食生产强势、部分矿产（铜、锂、锌）有储量但开采-精炼滞后

这种双峰结构意味着美国在"规则制定权"上占据绝对优势（可以让荷兰不卖EUV、让韩国限制HBM出口、让印度接受芯片外交），但在"把规则转化为自给能力"上严重滞后。一个典型矛盾：Entity List把华为锁在先进芯片之外，但F-35和核潜艇依赖中国稀土永磁。

四、结构性传导链

传导链1：台海危机 → AI产业停摆

台海危机（封锁、围岛演习升级、或武装冲突） ↓ TSMC先进制程产能瞬时归零（Nvidia H200/B200 GPU停产） ↓ 美国AI训练新模型能力冻结（现有GPU仍工作，但扩容停止） ↓ Arizona TSMC Phase 2未达量产（2027-2028才可能） ↓ 同时：HBM来自韩国（SK海力士+三星），台海冲突扰乱韩日供应链 ↓ AI产业供应链进入2-3年的"冻结期"——同期中国AI也受损但两国不对称

传导链2：中国稀土管制 → 国防与EV双击

北京扩大稀土/磁铁/镓/锗/锑/钨出口管制 ↓ F-35 NdFeB磁铁供应受限 → 生产线延迟（洛克希德已公开表达担忧） ↓ 弗吉尼亚级核潜艇磁铁受限（AUKUS承诺的2艘/年本已产能不足，现更紧） ↓ EV产业：特斯拉、福特、GM的电机稀土供应受限 ↓ 同时：中国石墨管制 → EV电池生产受影响（石墨是锂电池负极，100%中国精炼） ↓ IRA促建矿产加工产能需5-8年完工（MP Materials、Lynas 2028-2032） ↓ 2026-2030是"暴露窗口"——制裁已动但替代能力未起

传导链3：印度-中国医药依赖

中国对印度API出口管制（或因价格/物流问题断供） ↓ 印度74% API依赖中国 → 印度仿制药产能受限 ↓ 美国40%仿制药来自印度 → 美国医院系统基础药物短缺 ↓ 关键领域：抗生素（环丙沙星等发酵类API近100%依赖中国） ↓ 经典"两步传导"——美国对华关系表面上与此无关，但后果直接
这条链尤其重要：即使美国完全不与中国直接贸易，美国医药系统仍通过印度暴露于中国API。这是双边脱钩政策无法解决的"三边"问题——除非印度建立独立API产能（至少5年），否则所有"降低对华依赖"的叙事在医药领域是幻觉。

情景B：中国全面矿产武器化（2026-2028）

  • 触发条件：美国进一步扩大芯片管制（如EDA全面断供华为生态、扩大先进封装管制）
  • 传导：北京按梯度扩大稀土、镓、锗、锑、钨、石墨、锂管制。首月：价格飙升+美国SPR-equivalent金属储备释放；3-6月：武器生产延迟；12月：EV产业显著受阻

  • 结构性影响：美国被迫加速IRA执行，但无法在短期内弥补——2026-2028是"结构暴露期"

情景C：电力基建限制AI扩张（2026-2030，正在发生）

  • 触发条件：已发生 — 并网排队2.6TW+、变压器2-3年、燃气轮机到2029
  • 传导：超大规模用户（Microsoft、Amazon、Google、Meta）转向behind-the-meter发电 → 推高核电复兴、SMR投资、Gulf国家数据中心外迁
  • 落空条件：SMR商业化提前至2028；大规模电网投资法案通过
  • 结构性影响：美国可能拥有最先进的GPU但无法足够快地把它们部署到数据中心——这对与中国的AI竞赛形成隐性延迟。见AI物理约束报告详解

七、结构性评估

军事-企业复合体的再整合

Palantir、Anduril、SpaceX/Starshield代表硅谷与五角大楼的边界重新模糊，这是冷战后最重要的结构性转变。但这一转变有自身的产业基础约束：RTX（886亿营收，2680亿订单积压）等传统国防承包商的交付能力是限制因素。F-35的产能约1/艘月（但稀土供应压缩实际交付），弗吉尼亚级潜艇1艘/年（AUKUS需要2艘/年）。技术野心超前于制造产能是2026年美国国防战略的核心内部矛盾。

"盟友依赖性"作为隐藏资产

美国经常被分析为"孤立的帝国"。但在供应链MAD框架中，美国最大的资产恰恰是其盟友网络——没有荷兰（ASML）、日本（光刻胶、Zeiss合作）、德国（Zeiss光学）、韩国（HBM、存储），美国的技术优势就不存在。任何分析"美国能否独立供应自己"的叙事都误解了现代帝国的本质——帝国是联盟内部的分工系统，不是民族国家的独立能力。

与主题报告的交叉链接

  • 供应链MAD：美国的EDA + EUV + 美元系统构成技术否决权这一MAD支柱。但这是唯一随时间增强的杠杆——复利效应下2nm与中国7nm的差距不是线性缩小，而是指数扩大
  • 半导体六层迷宫：美国在第1层（设计）深度主导，第3-4层部分（设备、少数先进晶圆）保持领先，但第2层（材料）、第5-6层（先进封装/基板）不是美国强项。Arizona TSMC建厂解决第4层，但第2层（日本主导）和第5-6层（台日主导）的空洞不会因Arizona厂而消失
  • AI物理约束：美国的AI竞赛瓶颈已从芯片转到电力基础设施——变压器、燃气轮机、GOES电工钢。这些是美国建立最慢的领域

八、建议（按时间分层）

2026-2028 紧急（"填补空洞期"）

  • 稀土/镓/锗战略储备扩大至24-36个月供应。接受直接采购+补贴价格而非等待国内产能
  • 加速TSMC Arizona Phase 2、Samsung Taylor、Intel Ohio量产——接受良率和成本溢价
  • 激励印度本土API制造（USAID或贸易优惠）；在美国/墨西哥建立关键抗生素API产能
  • 与盟国协调SPR释放机制，应对霍尔木兹延续情景
  • 避免进一步升级芯片管制直至2027年（让中国矿产管制窗口衰减过去）

2028-2032 结构建设（"规模化替代"）

  • 矿产加工重心在铜、锂、石墨本土化；接受5-8年建设周期和~2x中国价格
  • 扩大电网投资：变压器、GOES电工钢、EHV输电。这是AI扩张的真正物理约束
  • 首批SMR商业化（NuScale、X-energy、Kairos）配套大型数据中心
  • 弗吉尼亚级产能加速至2艘/年，否则AUKUS承诺结构性不可兑现

2032+ 长期重构（"盟友协同的优势系统化"）

  • 将"盟友技术联盟"正式化——不再依靠非正式协调，建立类似CoCom的多边出口管制框架
  • 对北美+日本+韩国+台湾+欧洲建立关键矿产储备共同池
  • 接受美国作为"上端帝国"的定位，不追求全链自主——而是深化联盟内分工

United States Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

美国供应链脆弱性评估

Risk Score: 68/100 — NET VULNERABLE

Core Thesis

The United States is a "top-tier empire with a middle-tier hollow" — occupying the global apex in design (EDA, chip architecture, AI models), defense systems, energy commodities, and agricultural goods, but structurally hollowed out in the mid-tier manufacturing that turns those designs into physical products. Specifically: Washington writes the rules of global technology controls while being unable to manufacture the advanced chips its own AI industry requires; it holds the world's strongest military projection capability while the F-35 depends on Chinese rare earth magnets. Energy independence has been largely achieved, but the hollow period (2025-2030) in mineral processing and chip manufacturing coincides precisely with the peak period of supply chain weaponization (2027-2032). The $52 billion CHIPS Act and the $369 billion IRA are belated recognitions of the hollow — but insufficient to close it before it is exploited. This "rule-maker is the victim of the problem its rules cannot solve" structure is the central paradox of US strategy.

I. Structural Profile: Top Empire and Middle Hollow

America's industrial position is a unique bimodal distribution:

  • Peak: design and final integration — EDA (Synopsys + Cadence combined ~60% market), chip design (Nvidia, AMD, Apple, Broadcom, Qualcomm), AI models (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta), defense systems (RTX, Lockheed, Northrop), AI software stack
  • Valley: midstream manufacturing — advanced chip fabrication (TSMC Arizona capacity is <20% of domestic demand), critical mineral refining (90% dependent on imported processing), batteries, solar, bulk industrial chemicals, precision machinery components
  • Foundation: upstream resources — energy substantially self-sufficient (shale revolution), strong food production, partial mineral reserves (copper, lithium, zinc) but mining-to-refining lag

This bimodal structure means the US holds an absolute advantage in "rule-making" (able to force the Netherlands not to sell EUV, force Korea to restrict HBM exports, force India to accept chip diplomacy) but is severely behind in "converting rules into self-sufficiency." A paradigmatic contradiction: the Entity List locks Huawei out of advanced chips, but the F-35 and nuclear submarines depend on Chinese rare earth permanent magnets.

IV. Structural Transmission Chains

Chain 1: Taiwan Strait Crisis → AI Industry Halt

Taiwan Strait crisis (blockade, encirclement drill escalation, or armed conflict) ↓ TSMC advanced-node capacity instantly goes to zero (Nvidia H200/B200 GPU stops) ↓ US AI training of new models freezes (existing GPUs keep running, but expansion stops) ↓ Arizona TSMC Phase 2 not yet at volume (2027-2028 at earliest) ↓ Simultaneously: HBM comes from Korea (SK Hynix + Samsung); Taiwan Strait conflict disrupts Korea-Japan supply chains ↓ AI industry supply chain enters 2-3 year "freeze period" — China AI also damaged but the two are asymmetric

Chain 2: Chinese Rare Earth Controls → Double Hit to Defense and EVs

Beijing expands rare earth / magnet / gallium / germanium / antimony / tungsten export controls ↓ F-35 NdFeB magnet supply limited → production line delays (Lockheed has publicly voiced concern) ↓ Virginia-class nuclear submarine magnets limited (AUKUS commitment of 2/year already under-capacity, now tighter) ↓ EV industry: Tesla, Ford, GM motor rare earth supply limited ↓ Simultaneously: Chinese graphite controls → EV battery production hit (graphite is Li-ion anode, 100% Chinese refining) ↓ IRA-driven mineral processing capacity takes 5-8 years (MP Materials, Lynas 2028-2032) ↓ 2026-2030 is the "exposure window" — sanctions already fired but substitutes not yet up

Chain 3: India-China Pharmaceutical Dependency

China tightens API exports to India (or disruption for pricing/logistics reasons) ↓ India's 74% API dependency on China → Indian generic production constrained ↓ US's 40% of generic drugs from India → US hospital system essential drug shortage ↓ Critical zone: antibiotics (ciprofloxacin and other fermentation-based APIs ~100% China-dependent) ↓ Classic "two-step transmission" — US-China relations superficially unrelated, but consequences direct
This chain is especially important: even if the US ceased all direct trade with China, the US pharmaceutical system still flows through India to China API. This is a "triangular" problem bilateral decoupling policy cannot solve — unless India builds independent API capacity (minimum 5 years), every "reduce China dependency" narrative in pharma is illusion.

  • Transmission: Beijing expands rare earth, gallium, germanium, antimony, tungsten, graphite, lithium controls stepwise. First month: prices surge + US SPR-equivalent metal stockpile released; 3-6 months: weapons production delays; 12 months: EV industry significantly blocked
  • Falsification conditions: (i) China avoids escalation to preserve bargaining space; (ii) West accelerates alternative processing capacity online (but physically impossible within 5 years)
  • Structural consequences: US forced to accelerate IRA execution but cannot compensate in the short term — 2026-2028 is the "structural exposure window"

Scenario C: Power Infrastructure Limiting AI Expansion (2026-2030, Already Occurring)

  • Trigger: Already happened — interconnection queue 2.6TW+, transformers 2-3y, gas turbines to 2029
  • Transmission: Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta) pivot to behind-the-meter generation → push nuclear renaissance, SMR investment, Gulf state data center migration
  • Falsification conditions: SMR commercialization advances to 2028; mega grid investment act passes
  • Structural consequences: US may have the most advanced GPUs but cannot deploy them to data centers fast enough — an invisible delay in the AI race with China. See AI Physical Constraints report for details

VII. Structural Assessment

The Re-Integration of the Military-Enterprise Complex

Palantir, Anduril, SpaceX/Starshield represent the renewed blurring of the Silicon Valley / Pentagon boundary — the most important structural shift since the Cold War. But this shift has its own industrial-base constraints: traditional defense contractors like RTX ($88.6B revenue, $268B backlog) have delivery capacity as the limiting factor. F-35 production is ~1/month (but rare earth supply compresses actual delivery); Virginia-class submarine 1/year (AUKUS needs 2/year). Technology ambition outrunning manufacturing capacity is the central internal contradiction of 2026 US defense strategy.

"Allied Dependency" as Hidden Asset

The US is often analyzed as "the isolated empire." But in the Supply Chain MAD framework, America's greatest asset is precisely its alliance network — without the Netherlands (ASML), Japan (photoresist, Zeiss partnerships), Germany (Zeiss optics), South Korea (HBM, memory), the US technology edge does not exist. Any narrative analyzing whether "the US can supply itself independently" misunderstands the modern empire — the empire is the division-of-labor system within the alliance, not the independent capability of the nation-state.

VIII. Recommendations (By Time Horizon)

2026-2028 Emergency ("Fill the Hollow Period")

  • Rare earth / gallium / germanium strategic reserves expanded to 24-36 months of supply. Accept direct procurement + subsidy pricing rather than waiting for domestic capacity
  • Accelerate TSMC Arizona Phase 2, Samsung Taylor, Intel Ohio to volume — accept yield and cost premium
  • Incentivize Indian domestic API manufacturing (USAID or trade preferences); establish key antibiotic API capacity in the US/Mexico
  • Coordinate with allies on SPR release mechanism, addressing sustained Hormuz scenario
  • Avoid further chip control escalation until 2027 (let the Chinese mineral control window decay past)

2028-2032 Structural Build ("Substitution at Scale")

  • Mineral processing focus on copper, lithium, graphite localization; accept 5-8 year construction cycles and ~2x Chinese pricing
  • Expand grid investment: transformers, GOES electrical steel, EHV transmission. This is the real physical constraint on AI expansion
  • First SMR commercial operations (NuScale, X-energy, Kairos) paired with large data centers
  • Virginia-class capacity accelerated to 2/year, otherwise AUKUS commitment structurally unfulfillable

2032+ Long-Term Restructuring ("Systematize Alliance Coordination")

  • Formalize "allied technology alliance" — move beyond informal coordination to a CoCom-like multilateral export control framework
  • Establish a critical mineral reserve common pool for North America + Japan + Korea + Taiwan + Europe
  • Accept US position as "top-tier empire" without pursuing full-chain autarky — instead deepen division of labor within the alliance
