印度供应链脆弱性评估（2026）

India Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

风险评分：67/100 — 净脆弱 / NET VULNERABLE

核心论点

印度是"崛起中的组装者"——一个结构空洞的规模大国。劳动力规模（~9.9亿，已超越中国）和制造业抱负巨大，但产业基础严重空洞：74%药物API依赖中国、太阳能电池98%依赖中国、石油35%经霍尔木兹、国防核心平台依赖俄罗斯。印度最大的公共成就（"发展中世界的药房"——供应美国40%仿制药）是一个结构性虚假的主权——药物的最终剂型在印度制造但活性成分来自中国。这是"组装谬误"的教科书案例。印度的战略对冲策略（同时加入QUAD、上合组织、金砖+，进口折价俄油再精炼出口欧洲）在多极博弈中确实生效，带来短期利益——但长期仍受制于不控制关键投入这一结构事实。印度最具长期意义的机会是人口优势+英语世界准入+美国"去中国化"需求的组合，但能否抓住取决于能否在10年内把PLI计划从"进口替代游戏"转变为真正的产业升级。

六、情景分析

情景A：霍尔木兹持续封锁6+个月

  • 触发条件：已发生
  • 传导：沙特原油断供 → 俄罗斯原油补充部分缺口（已经运转）→ 成本上升 → 通胀压力（印度CPI敏感）→ 政治压力但可管理
  • 落空条件：霍尔木兹3月内重开 / 美伊协议
  • 结构性影响：俄印能源联系加深 → 与美国对印战略（对俄制裁执行）产生摩擦

情景B：中国对印API管制（2026-2028）

  • 触发条件：中印边境冲突升级、印度加入美国技术联盟、印度参与对华芯片管制
  • 传导：API断供 → 印度仿制药产能下降 → 对美药物出口减少 → 美国医院药物短缺（两步传导）+ 印度国内基础药物供应紧张 → 社会政治压力
  • 落空条件：(i) PLI加速至规模化；(ii) 中国避免使用以保留杠杆
  • 结构性影响：这是中国对印最有效的供应链武器——比稀土影响更直接、更快速

  • 落空条件：见CHN报告
  • 结构性影响：这是印度长期战略的关键情景——如果印度能在台海后成为"替代中国"的组装中心，其经济规模会跃升

七、结构性评估

"崛起中的组装者"——产缘政治框架的过渡形态

印度是产缘政治框架中最有趣的过渡案例——它在从"纯下游"（经典发展中国家）向"中游组装者"移动，但尚未成为真正的中游控制者（像德国、日本、韩国）。这一过渡的关键问题不是"GDP能增长多少"，而是能否在组装基础上建立上游能力。中国在1990-2010年间成功完成了这一跃升（从组装到材料加工到部分上游控制）；印度能否重演？

几个结构性因素使印度的跃升更难：

  • 劳动力优势但教育碎片化——印度大规模劳动力中受过良好工程训练的比例仍远低于中国同等发展阶段
  • 基础设施瓶颈——电力、港口、铁路仍滞后，影响工业规模扩张速度
  • 土地征用困难——土地法框架使大规模工厂建设比中国慢
  • 联邦结构——省级政策差异使全国统一产业政策难以执行

非站队作为战略——结构性可行性

印度的"多极对冲"策略（同时在QUAD、上合组织、金砖+中保持成员身份，同时进口俄油销往欧洲）在2026年运作良好。但这种策略的结构可行性取决于：

  • 美中冲突不升级到迫使选择站队的程度
  • 印度经济体量增长到让各方都需要印度合作
  • 印度不在任何具体危机中被要求做出公开表态

台海危机或霍尔木兹长期封锁都可能强制打破印度的多极平衡——德里将被迫选择站队，且任何选择都有代价。

与主题报告的交叉链接

  • 供应链MAD：印度是MAD中的"中间国家侵蚀者"——通过俄油转口、对华API依赖等，印度以实际行动说明二元脱钩叙事在结构上不可行
  • 半导体六层迷宫：印度在半导体上处于极早期（Vedanta/Tata-PSMC合资等）。10年内难以成为重要玩家
  • AI物理约束：印度AI电力基础设施正在被Reliance、Adani等私企建设，配合美国云服务商——可能成为亚洲次要AI基建中心

八、建议（按时间分层）

2026-2028 紧急

  • API国产化是印度最紧迫的单一供应链优先事项——PLI计划规模扩大3-5倍，加速时间表和专门化工业园建设
  • 太阳能电池+硅片制造一体化（从组装向上游）——与非中国厂商（First Solar、Meyer Burger）合资提供技术替代路径

India Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

印度供应链脆弱性评估

Risk Score: 67/100 — NET VULNERABLE

Core Thesis

India is the "rising assembler" — a structurally hollow scale power. Workforce size (~990M, surpassing China) and manufacturing ambition are enormous, but industrial foundation is severely hollow: 74% pharmaceutical APIs depend on China, 98% solar cells depend on China, 35% oil transits Hormuz, core defense platforms depend on Russia. India's biggest public achievement ("pharmacy of the developing world" — supplying 40% of US generics) is a structurally illusory sovereignty — drugs final dosage form manufactured in India but active ingredients from China. This is the textbook "assembly fallacy." India's strategic hedging (simultaneously in QUAD, SCO, BRICS+; importing discounted Russian oil, refining and re-exporting to Europe) works well in multipolar games, delivering short-term benefits — but remains long-term constrained by the structural fact of not controlling key inputs. India's most strategically significant opportunity is the combination of population advantage + English-world access + US "de-China" demand, but whether to seize it depends on whether PLI programs can, within 10 years, transform from "import substitution game" to genuine industrial upgrade.

The "rising assembler" trap: India's industrial growth model for the past 15 years has been assembly, not manufacturing — Apple iPhone assembly, Xiaomi OEM, solar assembly, pharmaceutical assembly. Assembly depends on two conditions: (a) upstream inputs (chips, APIs, photovoltaic cells) sustainably imported; (b) downstream markets (primarily US + Europe) open. Any condition wavering suspends the assembly advantage. This parallels China's early 2000s model, but with a key difference: China rapidly climbed from assembly to materials processing to partial upstream control in 1990-2010; India so far has climbed upstream slower than it has expanded assembly — the PLI scheme is testing whether this trajectory can change.

IV. Structural Transmission Chains

Chain 1: Pharmaceutical's Triangular Hollowness — India-China-US

India's "pharmacy of the developing world" infrastructure: 20% global generic volume + 40% US ↓ But 74% of India's APIs (active ingredients) from China Fermentation APIs (antibiotics like ciprofloxacin) ~100% China ↓ China tightens API exports to India → Indian generic capacity limited ↓ US hospital system essential drug shortage (via India transmission) ↓ Key implications: [a] Even if US fully decouples from China in trade, US pharma still exposed through India [b] Even if India expands "pharmacy of developing world" role, cannot claim "drug sovereignty" [c] Triangular vulnerability not solvable by bilateral decoupling policy

This triangular chain is the key node of US report's Chain 3. India's position appears to be "helping US reduce China dependency" (generics imports shifting from China to India), but actually hides bilateral dependency as triangular dependency.

Chain 2: Hormuz + Russian Oil Dual-Source Hedge

Hormuz blockade ↓ India's $35B Saudi crude directly cut ↓ India SPR only ~10 days (lowest among major economies) ↓ But India has important buffer: - Russian discounted crude $13B+ (via Black Sea/Pacific non-Hormuz) - Indian refineries refining Russian oil and partly re-exporting to Europe (legal arbitrage: once refined, "Indian product") ↓ This places India in better position than other Asian energy consumers (JP, KR) in Hormuz crisis ↓ But still faces: Saudi crude cannot be fully replaced by Russian (global market tight + transport bottleneck) ↓ Result: India won't suffer industrial shutdown like JP/KR, but energy costs rise notably

Chain 3: Solar's PLI Dilemma

India's ambitious solar targets (500 GW by 2030) ↓ Dependent on Chinese wafers (80%+) + cells (95%+) ↓ India PLI scheme (~$20B) targets building domestic module + cell + wafer manufacturing ↓ 2026 progress: - Module assembly: fast growth (Adani, Reliance, Waaree, etc.) - Cells: early stage (Adani 5GW and other projects online 2025-2026) - Wafers: near zero - Polysilicon: minimal ↓ Key question: China's scale cost advantage still enormous (even with tariffs) ↓ If PLI fails: India continues assembly but doesn't control upstream — energy transition strategically still China-dependent ↓ If PLI succeeds but delayed: China already leads in new tech (perovskite, HJT) → India catches up again

India's time double-edged sword: population and GDP growth provide time (through 2040 India is the main source of global population increase), but the "substitution speed" of nearly all supply chain key dimensions lags demand growth speed. E.g., solar localization to 30% takes 3 years, but in the same period India's solar capacity doubles — absolute domestic production grows, but share may not rise. This is the "treadmill problem" — to stay in place requires running faster.

VI. Scenarios

Scenario A: Sustained Hormuz Blockade 6+ Months

  • Trigger: Already occurred
  • Transmission: Saudi crude cut off → Russian crude partial backfill (already operating) → costs rise → inflation pressure (India CPI sensitive) → political pressure but manageable
  • Falsification conditions: Hormuz reopens within 3 months / US-Iran agreement
  • Structural consequences: Russia-India energy ties deepen → friction with US India strategy (anti-Russia sanctions enforcement)

  • Trigger: China-India border conflict escalates, India joins US tech alliance, India participates in China chip controls
  • Transmission: API cutoff → Indian generic capacity drops → US pharma exports decrease → US hospital drug shortage (two-step transmission) + India domestic essential drug supply tight → social political pressure
  • Falsification conditions: (i) PLI accelerates to scale; (ii) China avoids use to preserve leverage
  • Structural consequences: This is China's most effective supply chain weapon against India — more direct and faster than rare earth impact

Scenario C: Taiwan Strait Crisis (2027-2032)

  • Trigger: See CHN Scenario B
  • India-side transmission: (i) Global energy prices surge; (ii) Generic drug exports disrupted (China API cutoff); (iii) India can choose neutrality — theoretically a main beneficiary (absorbing supply chain relocation from China)
  • Falsification conditions: See CHN report
  • Structural consequences: This is India's key long-term strategic scenario — if India can become the "substitute China" assembly hub post-Taiwan, its economic scale would jump

VII. Structural Assessment

"Rising Assembler" — A Transition Form in the SCG Framework

India is the most interesting transition case in the supply chain geopolitics framework — moving from "pure downstream" (classic developing country) to "midstream assembler," but not yet becoming a genuine midstream controller (like Germany, Japan, Korea). The key question for this transition is not "how much GDP can grow," but whether upstream capability can be built on the assembly foundation. China successfully completed this leap in 1990-2010 (from assembly to materials processing to partial upstream control); can India replicate?

Several structural factors make India's leap harder:

  • Workforce advantage but fragmented education — the well-engineering-trained share of India's massive workforce still far below China's equivalent development stage
  • Infrastructure bottlenecks — power, ports, rail still lagging, affecting industrial scale expansion speed
  • Land acquisition difficulty — land law framework makes large-scale factory construction slower than in China
  • Federal structure — state-level policy differences make nationwide uniform industrial policy hard to implement

Non-alignment as Strategy — Structural Viability

India's "multipolar hedging" strategy (maintaining membership in QUAD, SCO, BRICS+ simultaneously, importing Russian oil and selling to Europe) functions well in 2026. But the structural viability of this strategy depends on:

  • US-China conflict not escalating to forced side-choosing
  • India's economic scale growing to make all sides need Indian cooperation
  • India not being demanded public position in any specific crisis

A Taiwan Strait crisis or prolonged Hormuz blockade could forcibly shatter India's multipolar balance — Delhi would be forced to choose, and any choice has costs.
