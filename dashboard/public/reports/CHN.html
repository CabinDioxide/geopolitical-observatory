中国供应链脆弱性评估（2026）

China Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

风险评分：83/100 — 净脆弱 / NET VULNERABLE

核心论点

中国占据一个结构性自我矛盾的位置：既是全球最大制造业经济体和关键矿产加工的上游控制者，又同时是海运石油、先进半导体、工业化所需铁矿石和蛋白质食物的深度下游依赖者。这一结构的内在不稳定性通过"三重绞杀"显现——人口坍塌、地缘政治围堵、供应链断裂三条下行曲线在2027-2032年同时加速。中国目前持有的矿产加工杠杆是其唯一真正的不对称工具，但该杠杆的时间半衰期短于结构性脆弱性的到期时间：矿产优势在2030年前后因西方替代产能上线而衰减，而先进芯片天花板和马六甲依赖是永久性约束。这种"杠杆衰减快于约束衰减"的不对称是理解北京激励结构的关键。

一、结构性画像

中国在全球生产网络中的位置不能用单一标签描述。它同时是：

  • 上游控制者（在关键矿产加工层）——稀土87%、钴80%、石墨100%、镓98%、锂60%全球精炼份额，这在供应链MAD框架中构成真正的非对称威慑
  • 中游巨无霸（在成熟制程半导体、电池组装、大宗化学品、太阳能组件等领域）——成熟制程（28nm+）全球产能约80%
  • 下游囚徒（在能源、先进芯片、铁矿、蛋白质食物层）——石油70%+进口、先进芯片几乎完全进口、铁矿石>70%进口、大豆80%+进口

这种"上游-下游同时"的异质结构是其他主要经济体没有的。它意味着任何单一维度的分析都会产生误导：只看上游会高估中国韧性，只看下游会低估中国反击能力。真实的战略计算必须同时权衡两个方向。

四、三重绞杀的传导链分析

把中国的脆弱性分解为三条独立但相互强化的传导链，比单一的"供应链风险"标签更准确。

绞杀链1：霍尔木兹 → 东亚半导体 → 中国工业

伊朗打击卡塔尔LNG设施 ↓ 卡塔尔氦气产能（全球1/3）下线 ↓ 韩国（64.7%氦气依赖卡塔尔）+ 台湾半导体制程受限 ↓ 三星/SK海力士HBM + TSMC先进制程减产 ↓ 中国从韩台进口的$80B+芯片（DRAM/NAND/先进逻辑）供应收缩 ↓ 中国AI、电动车、消费电子产能受阻 → 出口竞争力受损 ↓ 同时：中国从沙特$65B原油 + 卡塔尔$16B LNG直接断供 ↓ 国内工业电力/交通燃料紧张 → 通胀 + 社会压力

这条链的关键洞察：中国不需要霍尔木兹直接影响自己就会受损。氦气这条传导链是供应链MAD报告的第三方触发器机制的典型案例——通过与中国"表面无关"的中东冲突传导到中国核心产业。

绞杀链2：马六甲 → 能源 + 粮食 → 政权稳定

台海/马六甲封锁或保险市场拒保 ↓ 中国80%石油进口受阻（日消费~1500万桶中~1200万桶经此通道） ↓ 战略石油储备（约80天）被迅速消耗 ↓ +同时：巴西大豆（$36B）经印度洋/马六甲供应链受阻 ↓ 猪肉/禽肉价格飙升（饲料短缺） ↓ 食物通胀 → CPI冲击 → 社会稳定压力 ↓ (决策者层面)能源配给/粮食配给 → 经济活动减速 → 失业率上升

这条链的时间尺度很短：SPR从使用第一天起覆盖约80天，LNG储备约2-3周。中俄陆上管道（Power of Siberia、中缅、中哈）的理论产能不足以替代海运——即使全开也只能覆盖约30-40%的石油进口。

绞杀链3：技术封锁 → 先进制程 → AI/军工

美国EDA断供 + 对华ASML EUV禁令 + 日本光刻胶出口管制（2023起） ↓ 中芯国际被锁定在DUV多重曝光（~7nm级别） ↓ 每代制程差距复利扩大——2027年的2nm与中国7nm的鸿沟 >> 2024年的3nm与中国7nm ↓ 华为昇腾等AI芯片性能受限 ↓ 中国AI训练效率 vs. 美国差距扩大（每代GPU 1.5-2x性能提升复利） ↓ 国防：北斗、高超音速、隐形战机等系统的迭代速度受芯片限制

与前两条链不同，这条链没有时间终点——只要联盟架构（荷/日/韩/台追随美国）不崩溃，技术差距就会永久扩大。这是中国真正无法通过国内投资解决的约束。

中国的战略位置不是静止的——每个杠杆和约束都在不同的时间尺度上演化。2026年的"权力快照"掩盖了结构趋势：

六、情景分析

情景A：霍尔木兹持续封锁6+个月（正在发生）

  • 触发条件：已发生
  • 传导：SPR消耗→动用战备+伊朗灰色市场原油→沙特供给减半→俄罗斯/委内瑞拉补足约50%→成本上升+供应紧张持续
  • 落空条件（判断情景失效）：(i) 霍尔木兹在3个月内重新开放；(ii) 美伊达成临时协议；(iii) 俄罗斯大规模增产
  • 结构性影响：加速"东数西算"电力集中化，但加剧化石燃料依赖。副作用：俄罗斯议价权上升

情景B：台海危机（2027-2032窗口）

  • 触发条件：台湾独立公投、美舰过境升级、或大陆内部政治危机驱动"转移注意力"
  • 传导（四根MAD支柱同时激活）：
    1. 台积电停产 → 中国$52B芯片进口即刻归零 + 全球AI产业停转
    2. 台海航运切断 → 中国出口链受损（影响$500B+出口）
    3. 西方全面金融制裁（SWIFT踢出、美元资产冻结） → $3T+储备瞬时受冲击
    4. 联盟信誉传导 → 日韩欧加强对华技术封锁
  • 落空条件：(i) 2030年前北京明确放弃武统选项；(ii) 台湾重大政治转向（亲大陆党执政）；(iii) 中国内部经济崩溃优先于台海
  • 结构性影响：这是唯一一个同时激活中国所有四大脆弱性的情景。没有"部分失败"版本——一旦激活，传导全面且不可控

情景C：矿产武器过度使用 → 自我实现的脱钩（2026-2030）

  • 触发条件：北京判断"2030窗口关闭"并在此前发动全面矿产管制
  • 传导：每一次管制（钨→锑→镓→锗→锂→石墨）都加速西方投资替代产能 + 提高价格让替代项目ROI变好 + 推动盟友追随管制政策
  • 落空条件：西方无法在10年内建立替代加工产能；但现实中IRA + CRMA已在2028-2032年间陆续上线
  • 结构性影响：中国可能在2028-2030年"赢得"短期战术但丢失长期杠杆。每次管制是永久性的市场份额转移信号，一旦失去不可逆恢复

七、结构性评估

"三个中国"与产业基础

理解中国供应链能力必须区分哈耶克中国（长三角/珠三角，自发秩序涌现的制造业生态，2.5-3亿人口）、基层中国（约10亿人口，内需消费基础）、共产党中国（北京政治决策层）。供应链主权的物质基础来自哈耶克中国——但对其战略运用的决定来自共产党中国。这种"生产者与决策者不同身份"是中国供应链政策的根本张力——每一次产业政策干预都有可能破坏产生这种能力的自发秩序本身。

组装工厂而非制造工厂

西方分析中常见的"中国不可替代"叙事有一个根本误解：中国主要是组装的枢纽，而非从原材料到终端的全链制造枢纽。组装的规模依赖两个条件：(a) 能源+原材料进口顺畅；(b) 出口市场开放。任何一个条件被冲击，组装优势就悬空。这就是为什么石油海运依赖和市场准入风险是中国的两大结构性风险，而不是"人口红利消失"——组装模式在人口减少前就会因上述两个条件恶化而崩溃。

与主题报告的交叉链接

  • 供应链MAD：中国持有的矿产加工杠杆是MAD的主要中国方支柱。但其三个结构性差异（异质/时变/无共识刻度）对中国尤为不利——矿产杠杆时变，技术约束加强，升级无共识刻度意味着每次矿产管制都可能触发误读
  • 半导体六层迷宫：中国在六层中的分布极不均衡——基础材料和成熟设备接近自立，先进封装差距可能缩小，但EUV和顶级EDA结构上不可能在本十年赶上
  • AI物理约束：中国的AI瓶颈恰好与美国相反——电力充足（东数西算可利用）但芯片受限。这种不对称意味着中美AI竞赛不会有"一方速胜"

八、建议（按时间分层）

2026-2028 紧急（"持住杠杆"）

  • 加速马六甲绕行：缅甸皎漂管道 + 巴基斯坦瓜达尔产能扩张至国内石油需求的30-35%
  • SPR目标从80天提升至120+天，应对同时发生的霍尔木兹+台海情景
  • 克制矿产武器的过度使用——每次管制都在永久削弱未来的议价空间
  • 江西/广东国内离子型稀土开采增产，对冲缅甸边境不稳定

2028-2032 结构调整（"杠杆替代"）

  • 把战略重心从"抢夺先进制程"转向"巩固成熟制程"——成熟制程是唯一可持续增长的杠杆
  • 通过扩大对巴西、俄罗斯、东南亚的长期粮食/能源合约，降低食物通胀风险
  • 对印尼镍、刚果钴的持续投资锁定资源端而非精炼端杠杆
  • 与Samsung、SK海力士建立直接股权关系（中国车厂+韩国电池厂）锁定联盟脆弱性

2032+ 结构性对冲（"人口+债务"）

  • 自动化密度继续提升以对冲劳动力下降，但需认识到机器人不消费——必须同时建立消费保障
  • 长期债务重组，避免地方平台级联违约
  • 准备生育率持续低于1.0的场景——不是"如何提高"，而是"低出生率下的社会组织"

China Supply Chain Vulnerability Assessment (2026)

中国供应链脆弱性评估

Risk Score: 83/100 — NET VULNERABLE

Core Thesis

China occupies a structurally self-contradictory position: simultaneously the world's largest manufacturing economy and the upstream controller of critical mineral processing, yet also a deep downstream dependent for seaborne oil, advanced semiconductors, industrial iron ore, and protein. The instability of this structure manifests through "triple strangulation" — demographic collapse, geopolitical encirclement, and supply chain disruption — three downward curves accelerating simultaneously in the 2027-2032 window. China's mineral processing leverage is its only genuine asymmetric tool, but the time half-life of that leverage is shorter than the expiration date of its structural vulnerabilities: mineral dominance decays by 2030 as Western substitute capacity comes online, while advanced-chip ceilings and the Malacca dependency are permanent. This "leverage decaying faster than constraints" asymmetry is the key to understanding Beijing's incentive structure.

I. Structural Profile

China's position in the global production network cannot be captured by a single label. It is simultaneously:

  • Upstream controller (critical mineral processing) — 87% rare earths, 80% cobalt, 100% graphite, 98% gallium, 60% lithium global refining shares, constituting the genuine asymmetric deterrent in the Supply Chain MAD framework
  • Midstream juggernaut (mature-node semiconductors, battery assembly, bulk chemicals, solar modules) — about 80% of global mature-node (28nm+) capacity
  • Downstream prisoner (energy, advanced chips, iron ore, protein) — 72% oil imports, near-total advanced chip imports, >70% iron ore imports, 80%+ soybean imports

This "upstream-and-downstream simultaneously" heterogeneous structure is unique among major economies. It means any single-dimensional analysis misleads: looking only upstream overestimates Chinese resilience; looking only downstream underestimates Chinese counterattack capability. Genuine strategic calculus must weigh both directions simultaneously.

IV. Triple Strangulation: Transmission Chain Analysis

Decomposing Chinese vulnerability into three independent but mutually reinforcing transmission chains is more accurate than the single label of "supply chain risk."

Strangulation 1: Hormuz → East Asian Semiconductors → Chinese Industry

Iran strikes Qatar LNG facilities ↓ Qatari helium capacity (1/3 of global) offline ↓ South Korea (64.7% helium from Qatar) + Taiwan semiconductor processes constrained ↓ Samsung/SK Hynix HBM + TSMC advanced nodes reduce output ↓ China's $80B+ chip imports from Korea/Taiwan (DRAM/NAND/advanced logic) contract ↓ Chinese AI, EV, consumer electronics capacity impeded → export competitiveness damaged ↓ Simultaneously: $65B Saudi crude + $16B Qatari LNG to China directly cut off ↓ Domestic industrial power/transport fuel tight → inflation + social pressure

The key insight: China doesn't need to be directly affected by Hormuz to suffer. The helium chain is a paradigmatic case of the third-party trigger mechanism in the Supply Chain MAD report — transmitting through a Middle East conflict "superficially unrelated" to China into China's core industries.

Strangulation 2: Malacca → Energy + Food → Regime Stability

Taiwan Strait/Malacca blockade or insurance market refusal ↓ 80% of China's oil imports disrupted (~12M of ~15M bpd via this channel) ↓ Strategic petroleum reserves (~80 days) rapidly consumed ↓ + Simultaneously: Brazilian soybeans ($36B) supply chain via Indian Ocean/Malacca impeded ↓ Pork/poultry prices surge (feed shortage) ↓ Food inflation → CPI shock → social stability pressure ↓ (Decision-maker level) Energy rationing/food rationing → economic slowdown → rising unemployment

This chain's timescale is short: SPR covers roughly 80 days from day one of use, LNG reserves 2-3 weeks. China-Russia/Myanmar/Kazakhstan overland pipelines have theoretical capacity insufficient to replace maritime flows — even fully open, they cover only 30-40% of oil imports.

Strangulation 3: Tech Blockade → Advanced Nodes → AI/Military

US EDA cutoff + ASML EUV export ban to China + Japanese photoresist controls (from 2023) ↓ SMIC locked at DUV multi-patterning (~7nm equivalent) ↓ Each generation process gap compounds — 2027's 2nm vs China's 7nm gap >> 2024's 3nm vs China's 7nm ↓ Huawei Ascend and other AI chips performance-limited ↓ China AI training efficiency vs US gap widens (compounding effect of each GPU generation's 1.5-2x) ↓ Defense: iteration speed of BeiDou, hypersonics, stealth aircraft limited by chips

Unlike the first two, this chain has no time endpoint — as long as the alliance architecture (NL/JP/KR/TW following the US) doesn't collapse, the technology gap will permanently widen. This is the real constraint China cannot solve via domestic investment.

VI. Scenarios

Scenario A: Prolonged Hormuz Blockade, 6+ months (Ongoing)

  • Trigger: Already occurred
  • Transmission: SPR drawdown → reserve deployment + Iranian gray-market crude → Saudi supply halved → Russia/Venezuela replace ~50% → cost rise + supply tight persists
  • Falsification conditions (signs the scenario is failing): (i) Hormuz reopens within 3 months; (ii) US-Iran interim deal; (iii) Russia ramps up production at scale
  • Structural consequences: Accelerates East-Data-West-Compute power centralization but deepens fossil dependency. Side effect: Russian bargaining power rises

Scenario B: Taiwan Strait Crisis (2027-2032 window)

  • Trigger: Taiwan independence referendum, US vessel transit escalation, or PRC internal political crisis driving "attention diversion"
  • Transmission (all four MAD pillars activate simultaneously):
    1. TSMC halts → China's $52B chip imports instantly zero + global AI industry stops
    2. Taiwan Strait shipping cut → Chinese export chain damaged ($500B+ exports affected)
    3. Full Western financial sanctions (SWIFT cutoff, dollar asset freeze) → $3T+ reserves instantly shocked
    4. Alliance credibility transmission → Japan/Korea/EU tighten China tech blockade
  • Falsification conditions: (i) Beijing explicitly renounces military unification before 2030; (ii) Taiwan major political turn (pro-mainland party in power); (iii) Chinese internal economic collapse prioritizes before Taiwan
  • Structural consequences: The only scenario activating all four Chinese vulnerabilities simultaneously. No "partial failure" version — once triggered, transmission is comprehensive and uncontrollable

Scenario C: Mineral Weapon Overuse → Self-fulfilling Decoupling (2026-2030)

  • Trigger: Beijing judges "2030 window closing" and launches comprehensive mineral controls before then
  • Transmission: Each control (W → Sb → Ga → Ge → Li → graphite) accelerates Western investment in substitute capacity + raises prices making alternative project ROI better + pushes allies to follow control policies
  • Falsification conditions: The West cannot establish substitute processing capacity within 10 years; but in reality IRA + CRMA are coming online 2028-2032
  • Structural consequences: China may "win" short-term tactics in 2028-2030 but lose long-term leverage. Each control is a permanent market share transfer signal — once lost, irreversibly unrecoverable

VII. Structural Assessment

"Three Chinas" and the Industrial Base

Understanding Chinese supply chain capacity requires distinguishing Hayekian China (Yangtze/Pearl Delta, the self-organized manufacturing ecosystem of 250-300M), Grassroots China (~1B population, the domestic consumption base), and Communist Party China (Beijing's political leadership). The material basis of supply chain sovereignty comes from Hayekian China — but decisions on its strategic deployment come from Communist Party China. This "producer-and-decision-maker at different identities" asymmetry is the fundamental tension of Chinese supply chain policy — every industrial policy intervention risks damaging the self-organized order that produced the capability in the first place.

Assembly Hub, Not Manufacturing Hub

Western analysis often contains the fundamental misconception of "China irreplaceability." In truth, China is primarily an assembly hub, not a raw-material-to-end-product full chain manufacturing hub. Assembly scale depends on two conditions: (a) smooth energy + raw material imports; (b) open export markets. If either condition is shocked, assembly advantage hangs in air. This is why seaborne oil dependency and market access risk are China's two structural risks, not "demographic dividend loss" — the assembly model would collapse from the above two conditions deteriorating before population decline even hits.
